Welcome to The Sharing Project website!

As a high school IB student, I learned about the power of philanthropy and how one doesn't have to be rich to help others. In planning my Service Learning Project, I was hoping to bring together my passion for healthy nutrition and experience with gardening to benefit others.

Food insecurity is one of the persistent global challenges and it’s disheartening to think that over 690 million people globally and over 40 million Americans face hunger every day. To help our community members in need, I have founded The Sharing Project - a Non-Profit organization with the mission to grow and donate fresh, healthy food through a partnership of high schoolers with local gardening & farming communities. I reached out to my fellow student Matt Goldgrish and offered him to join efforts. Through community outreach we established contacts with food pantries to ascertain the need for fresh produce - it was very high. The COVID-19 pandemic had a tremendous negative impact on local businesses. Chester Mendham Food Pantry (CMFP) supported 25 families over the past couple of years, but in spring of 2020, 40 families found themselves in need and by the fall the number grew to over 60. A similar pattern was experienced at the Interfaith Food Pantry. Their customer base grew from 1000 to 2000 people over the past summer and it continues to grow.

Thus I saw even greater value in delivering healthy food to them. Better nutrition translates into a healthier body and stronger immunity. This was exactly what we needed to help prevent further expansion of the virus and that was and remains to be a big motivator for me. I engaged the local community: 9 student volunteers and 18 local gardeners teamed up to grow vegetables to address the increasing demand for food. More than 700 seedlings have been planted and cared for in 6 gardens. Starting in June, we harvested and donated fresh produce on a regular basis (in total over 600 lb).

What’s next? The Sharing Project and our drive grew with every produce delivery and the positive feedback we received from the CMFP. We worked closely with the Mendham Township Committee and obtained their support of the proposal to build The Sharing Project Garden on the former Mount Pleasant baseball field. 

More volunteers have already joined to help build the garden and ensure the continuity of the effort after the current seniors enroll in college next year. The Sharing Project was registered as a non-profit organization and is ready to engage with new individual and corporate partnerships. We are aiming to raise enough money to build a fence around the Mount Pleasant field, bring water piping, build garden beds, and purchase tools and supplies for next spring. There is enough space to establish a plentiful garden to grow produce for all clients of the Chester Mendham and Interfaith Food Pantries, and we are motivated to do it!

Would you like to learn more and get involved? We would appreciate any support our neighbors can provide, so together we can grow and share the joy of gardening while helping others.

With warm regards,
Matthew Borinshteyn
Founder and Chairman of the Board

FOOD INSECURITY FACTS

“Around the world, 690 million people regularly go to bed hungry, according to a report from the United Nations food agencies. The State of Food Insecurity and Nutrition in the World 2020 — released by the Food and Agriculture Organization, the International Fund for Agricultural Development, UNICEF, the World Food Programme, and the World Health Organization — reports that the number of people affected by hunger in the world continues to increase”.  
https://www.worldvision.org/hunger-news-stories/world-hunger-facts  

 
Even in the world’s greatest food-producing nation, children and adults face poverty and hunger in every county across America.  
  • In 2019, 34 million people lived in poverty in America. For a family of four, that means earning just $25,000 per year.
  • Before the coronavirus pandemic, more than 35 million people struggled with hunger in the United States, including more than 10 million children.
  • A household that is food insecure has limited or uncertain access to enough food to support a healthy life.
  • Children are more likely to face food insecurity than any other group in the United States. 
  • The coronavirus pandemic has left millions of families without stable employment.
  • More than 50 million people, including 17 million children, may experience food insecurity in 2020.
https://www.feedingamerica.org/hunger-in-america/facts
